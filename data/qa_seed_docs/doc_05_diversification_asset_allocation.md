# Diversification and Asset Allocation

Asset allocation is the split of a portfolio across broad asset classes (for example equities, bonds, and cash).  
Diversification spreads risk across holdings so one position has less impact on total results.

## Core principles

- Allocation is usually a bigger driver of risk than picking individual securities.
- Diversification reduces unsystematic (company-specific) risk.
- It does not remove systematic market risk.

## Common approaches

- **Strategic allocation**: target weights (e.g., 60/40) with periodic rebalancing.
- **Lifecycle allocation**: risk level shifts with time horizon.
- **Core-satellite**: broad diversified core + smaller thematic sleeves.

## Rebalancing basics

- Rebalancing restores target weights after market moves.
- It can enforce discipline by trimming winners and adding to laggards.

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Can I diversify away all risk?**  
  A: No, broad market risk remains.

- **Q: Is holding many stocks always diversified?**  
  A: Not necessarily. If holdings are concentrated in one sector or factor, risk can still be high.
