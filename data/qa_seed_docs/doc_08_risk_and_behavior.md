# Risk, Volatility, and Behavioral Biases

Risk is uncertainty about outcomes; volatility is one way to measure how much returns fluctuate.

## Risk concepts

- **Volatility**: variability of returns over time.
- **Drawdown**: decline from a prior peak.
- **Liquidity risk**: difficulty selling without moving price.
- **Concentration risk**: too much exposure to one asset, sector, or factor.

## Behavioral biases

- **Loss aversion**: losses often feel more painful than equivalent gains feel good.
- **Recency bias**: recent events overweight long-term judgment.
- **Overconfidence**: overestimating forecasting skill.
- **Herd behavior**: following crowd behavior in stressed markets.

## Practical controls

- Predefined allocation and rebalancing rules
- Position sizing limits
- Diversification and cash planning
- Written investment policy or checklist

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Is volatility the same as permanent loss?**  
  A: No. Volatility is movement; permanent loss depends on whether value fails to recover.

- **Q: Why do people buy high and sell low?**  
  A: Emotional reactions and biases can override long-term plans.
