# Profitability and Valuation Ratios

Ratios help compare companies across size and time.

## Profitability ratios

- **Gross margin** = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- **Operating margin** = Operating income / Revenue
- **Net margin** = Net income / Revenue
- **ROE** = Net income / Shareholders' equity
- **ROA** = Net income / Total assets

## Valuation ratios

- **P/E** = Price per share / Earnings per share
- **P/S** = Market value / Revenue
- **EV/EBITDA** compares enterprise value to operating cash earnings proxy

## Interpretation cautions

- A low P/E can signal undervaluation or weaker growth prospects.
- Ratios differ by industry; compare within similar business models.
- One-time gains or losses can distort earnings-based ratios.

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Is a high ROE always good?**  
  A: Not always. High leverage can inflate ROE.

- **Q: Why use EV/EBITDA instead of P/E sometimes?**  
  A: EV/EBITDA is less affected by capital structure and some accounting differences.
