# Mutual Funds, ETFs, and Index Investing

Funds pool investor money into diversified portfolios.

## Main vehicles

- **Mutual funds**: typically priced once per day at net asset value.
- **ETFs**: trade on exchanges during market hours.
- **Index funds**: track a benchmark index instead of active stock selection.

## Why investors use them

- Instant diversification
- Operational simplicity
- Often lower cost for passive index strategies

## Cost concepts

- **Expense ratio**: annual fund operating cost as a percentage.
- **Tracking difference**: gap between fund return and index return.
- **Turnover**: higher turnover can increase trading frictions and taxes (jurisdiction dependent).

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Are ETFs always better than mutual funds?**  
  A: Not always. Choice depends on costs, tax context, trading needs, and fund quality.

- **Q: What is index investing trying to achieve?**  
  A: Broad market exposure at low cost, rather than attempting to consistently beat the market.
