# Retirement Planning Basics

Retirement planning estimates how much to save and how to invest over a long horizon.

## Core inputs

- Target retirement age
- Expected spending needs
- Inflation assumptions
- Expected return assumptions
- Withdrawal strategy in retirement

## Building blocks

- Regular contributions over time
- Tax-advantaged accounts where available (country-specific)
- Periodic allocation review and rebalancing
- Emergency reserves separate from long-term investments

## Longevity and sequence risk

- **Longevity risk**: outliving assets.
- **Sequence-of-returns risk**: poor returns early in withdrawal years can materially reduce sustainability.

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Why is inflation critical in retirement models?**  
  A: Spending needs generally rise over decades, reducing real purchasing power.

- **Q: Should retirement plans be static?**  
  A: No. Plans should be reviewed as income, expenses, and goals change.
