# Credit Scores and Debt Management Fundamentals

Credit profile and debt structure influence borrowing access and cost.

## Credit score drivers (framework differs by bureau/country)

- Payment history
- Credit utilization
- Length of credit history
- Credit mix
- New credit inquiries

## Debt health indicators

- **Debt-to-income ratio**: debt obligations relative to income.
- **Interest coverage (household perspective)**: ability to service payments from regular cash flow.
- **Effective rate**: total borrowing cost, including fees where applicable.

## Practical debt management

- Prioritize high-interest debt repayment.
- Avoid persistent revolving balances where possible.
- Keep utilization moderate.
- Build an emergency fund to avoid crisis borrowing.

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Does checking my own credit score hurt my score?**  
  A: In many systems, soft checks typically do not affect scores; hard inquiries may.

- **Q: Is all debt bad?**  
  A: Not necessarily. Sustainability depends on cost, purpose, and repayment capacity.
