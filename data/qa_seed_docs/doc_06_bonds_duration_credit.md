# Bond Basics: Yield, Duration, and Credit Risk

Bonds are debt instruments where investors lend money to an issuer in exchange for interest and principal repayment.

## Core terms

- **Coupon**: periodic interest payment.
- **Yield**: return measure based on price and cash flows.
- **Maturity**: date principal is repaid.
- **Duration**: sensitivity of bond price to interest-rate changes.
- **Credit risk**: chance the issuer misses payments.

## Key relationships

- When market interest rates rise, existing bond prices typically fall.
- Longer duration generally means greater rate sensitivity.
- Lower credit quality usually requires higher yield compensation.

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Why do bond prices move if coupons are fixed?**  
  A: New bonds may offer different rates, so old bonds reprice to stay competitive.

- **Q: Is yield the same as coupon?**  
  A: No. Coupon is fixed in the bond terms; yield depends on market price.
