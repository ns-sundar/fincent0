# Financial Statements Basics

Financial statements answer three common questions:

1. **What does a company own and owe?** -> balance sheet
2. **Did it earn a profit over a period?** -> income statement
3. **Where did cash come from and go?** -> cash flow statement

## Core components

- **Balance sheet**: assets, liabilities, and equity at a point in time.
- **Income statement**: revenue, expenses, and net income over a period.
- **Cash flow statement**: operating, investing, and financing cash flows.

## Why all three matter together

- A company can report accounting profit but weak cash generation.
- A strong balance sheet can help absorb downturns.
- Cash flow trends often reveal business quality better than a single quarter of earnings.

## Common Q&A snippets

- **Q: Is net income the same as cash?**  
  A: No. Net income is accounting-based; cash flow tracks actual cash movement.

- **Q: Why does depreciation reduce earnings but not cash immediately?**  
  A: Depreciation is a non-cash accounting expense representing asset wear over time.

- **Q: What is retained earnings?**  
  A: Cumulative profits kept in the business rather than paid out as dividends.
